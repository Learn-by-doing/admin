Privacy Hacking

Testing the limits of privacy invasion tools to better understand how to protect ourselves in a post-privacy world

"Stalker Booth"

A physical art piece that will give people the chance to see how much of their privacy they voluntarily give away by irresponsibly sharing data online.

How it will work

  1. The person will enter the booth
  2. A camera will take a single photo of the person
  3. A reverse image search will find the person's publicly available profiles
  4. Interesting data will be gathered about the person: name, email, phone number, friends, places they have been recently
  5. The information will be displayed on a screen
  6. Give advice for how they can protect their privacy in the future

Step by Step

To improve our chances of success, we can divide the project into parts.

Reverse Image Search

Automate the process of finding the name, email, or profile of a person from only a picture of their face

Profile Enumeration

Using the first bits of information we found above, we will search for more

Visual Display

How do we present the data in the most clear, shocking way possible?
